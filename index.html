About the Club

Championing a remarkable breed since 2009

The club was founded eight years after the first three Entlebuchers were introduced to the UK. Since then, through careful import and a clear breeding programme, our registered population has grown steadily.

We operate within strict breeding guidelines to ensure the health and character of all dogs conforms to the high standards upheld in their native Switzerland. Every breeding dog undergoes a rigorous approval process — the Ankörung — essential to maintaining and promoting the breed's well-being.

The Breed

The Smallest Swiss Mountain Dog

An affable, loyal, and incredibly intelligent companion — originally bred to herd cattle and pull milk carts across the Swiss Alps.

Temperament

Bright, engaging, and incredibly loyal. They develop particularly strong attachments to their prime carer and are wonderfully affable family dogs — though their enthusiastic affection at 23–30kg can leave a bruise or two!

Exercise & Care

Moderately demanding of exercise but don't be fooled — you'll tire before they do. They love free-running, agility trials, and flyball. Incredibly clean dogs, they take pride in their appearance with minimal grooming needed.

Companionship

They form a very strong bond with their principal carer and thrive on human company. They're not suited to being left alone for long periods. In return, you'll have the most devoted companion imaginable.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common Questions About Entlebucher Mountain Dogs

What is an Entlebucher Mountain Dog?

The Entlebucher Mountain Dog is the smallest of the four Swiss Mountain Dog breeds. They weigh between 23–30kg and are known for their tricolour coat (black, tan, and white), loyal temperament, and exceptional intelligence. Originally bred to herd cattle and pull milk carts in the Swiss Alps, they make devoted family companions who form particularly strong bonds with their primary carer.

How many Entlebucher Mountain Dogs are there in the UK?

As of 2026, there are 136 registered Entlebucher Mountain Dogs in the UK. The first three were introduced in 2001, and numbers have grown through careful import and a structured breeding programme. The breed was placed on the Kennel Club Import Register in 2006 and received an Interim Breed Standard in 2013.

How do I get an Entlebucher puppy in the UK?

Contact the EMDCGB Puppy Secretary at [email protected] to join the puppy waiting list. The club arranges both UK-born puppies and imports from reputable European breeders. All breeding dogs undergo rigorous health testing including PRA, hip scoring, heart screening, eye tests, and ectopic ureter screening, plus the Swiss Ankörung approval process.

What health tests do Entlebucher breeders carry out?

EMDCGB-approved breeders test for six key conditions: Progressive Retinal Atrophy (PRA), Cataracts, Glaucoma (via gonioscopy), Heart conditions (cardiac screening), Hip Dysplasia (X-ray scoring from A to E), and Ectopic Ureter (ultrasound). These follow the requirements of the Swiss Breed Club, which has overall responsibility for the breed worldwide.

Are Entlebucher Mountain Dogs good family pets?

Yes — Entlebuchers have an affable temperament that makes them excellent family dogs. They are bright, engaging, and incredibly loyal. However, they develop very strong bonds with their principal carer and are not suited to being left alone for long periods. They are moderately demanding of exercise, enjoy agility and flyball, and are remarkably clean dogs requiring little grooming.
