Health Testing

Every breed has its particular health issues. Responsible breeders reduce risk by ensuring their breeding dogs have had all recommended health checks — and so should you when choosing a puppy.

Our Commitment

Rigorous Testing for Healthier Dogs

Here at the EMDCGB, we take health testing very seriously. We insist our breeders' dogs have passed all of the tests required by the Swiss Breed Club, which has overall responsibility for the breed. Any breeder approved by the EMDCGB will be happy to share the results of the tests for their puppies' parents with you on request.

Buyer beware: If you are buying a puppy and the breeder is unable or unwilling to show the results of all required tests for both parents, we would ask you to think carefully before proceeding — or speak to us for advice.

Health Conditions

What We Test For

Summaries of the health conditions for which the Club requires UK breeding dogs to test, following the requirements of the Swiss Breed Club.

Progressive Retinal Atrophy

PRA is a range of eye conditions in which the retina degenerates, leading to blindness. Only one type is known within Entlebuchers and all breeding dogs are tested. Any dog shown to have inherited the defective gene from both parents — and who will at some point develop the condition — will never be used for breeding by EMDCGB members.

Learn more

Cataracts

It's unusual for a cataract to be a significant problem for a dog, and small cataracts are often barely noticeable. Because some forms found in Entlebuchers are thought to be hereditary, some countries exclude all affected dogs from breeding. We follow those countries (including Switzerland) that allow breeding from a dog with a single small cataract in one eye.

Learn more

Glaucoma

Glaucoma is the build-up of fluid in the eye due to drainage problems, which can lead to painful headaches, blindness, and usually loss of the eye. There is no definitive genetic test, so the best option is a gonioscopy — a painless eye test that provides a strong indicator of risk. The UK Club works with other European clubs to assess the risk for breeding dogs.

Learn more

Heart

In order to prevent genetic heart conditions being passed on and to protect the health and welfare of breeding dogs, any dogs which show a heart murmur do not go on to breed. All breeding dogs are screened by a qualified veterinary cardiologist.

Learn more

Hips

Entlebuchers can suffer from Hip Dysplasia, like many breeds. While it can be hereditary, it can also be caused by injury, over-exercise at a young age, or diet. Dogs in the UK breeding programme are X-rayed at around 15 months and scored from A (best) to E (worst). Breeding from dogs graded D or E is not acceptable. To date, no UK Entlebucher has scored worse than B2.

Learn more

Ectopic Ureter

Ectopic ureter is a condition where the line from each kidney to the bladder enters in the wrong place, causing frequent infections, incontinence, and potentially death. Female puppies with this condition are unlikely to survive early puppyhood. It can be detected through ultrasound. The UK Club does not breed from affected dogs.

Learn more
