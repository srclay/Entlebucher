Our Grey Noses

Meet some of our most senior and beloved club members — all over 10 years old and much loved family pets. Their stories remind us why this breed is so special.

Wilma

Born 25th July 2015 Grey Nose

Wilma vom Rickental — "Wild Wilma" to her friends — is the most wonderful dog a human could ever want. A highly emotionally intelligent girl who takes care of her humans to the very best of her ability. She loves steak, ice cream, and vanilla biscuits.

Born in Switzerland, she still enjoys regular trips to her homeland and once had the privilege of visiting the Swiss Embassy in London to meet the Ambassador. These days she's more often found mooching around the garden, eating apples from the orchard, and staying close to her humans.

Cleo

Born 5th April 2013 Grey Nose

Cleo is a veteran retired trainee search and rescue dog. She retired when her human decided to have a family and could no longer go out searching at all hours of the night. But that's OK — it leaves more time for enjoyable forest and beach walks, and more importantly, energy to keep her Appenzeller "brother" in close check. And of course, plenty of naps!

Cinders

Born 28th December 2013 Grey Nose

Cinders (Cinderella) is from Shadow's C litter. It's hard to think she has owned her humans for over 10 years, but she's still very much a puppy at heart — even though her legs don't work quite as well as they used to.

She is as opinionated and sassy as ever and still loves nothing more than a day out exploring, but equally loves curling up for cuddles. Her absolute favourite day is a trip to the beach followed by an ice cream. She now has arthritis in her paws which means boots whenever she goes out — but with them on she's still unstoppable!

Soggi

Born 19th September 2011 Grey Nose

Soggi (formerly Annette) is from Shadow's first litter, Aristotle's sister. Still Soggi by name, Soggy by nature — she loves everything to do with water, except the sea, which she has been ignoring for over a decade! Digging up pebbles on the beach and in the river remains her favourite game.

Food, of course, is most important to her — she enjoys fresh muesli with fruit and natural yoghurt for breakfast, and pasta with raw vegetables and an egg for dinner. She has become deaf, which has increased her anxiety about not being with her humans at all times, but on the bright side she barks less at passers-by!

Have a Grey Nose of your own? We'd love to feature them here. Send photos and your story to the Membership Secretary.
